Engineering dossier

The systems brief straight from Ängelholm.

Three pillars shape every validation run: active aerodynamics, thermal resilience, and track intelligence. Use this briefing as the reference for every call you make inside the console.

Active control stack

SmartCluster samples yaw, pitch, and wind shear at 600 Hz to trim drag or add yaw authority. The simulator models these corrections when you shift aero balance or increase risk tolerance.

Thermal headroom

Koenigsegg’s triplex dampers share loads with the battery buffer. Push ERS deployments too hard and the system will clip power above 135°C. Balance outright speed with the heat load you can sustain.

Track intelligence

Every proving ground feeds friction coefficients, wind profiles, and surface temperature data. Recon options in the console replicate how the team validates grip before each certification attempt.

Innovation threads

From aero labs to power electronics.

Validation isn’t just a straight-line chase. Hyper-Carz pulls R&D insights from the carbon lab, aero tunnels, and data science teams. These programmes influence the sliders you control inside the console.

Carbon intelligence

Ultrasonic layup scans measure stiffness deltas across the monocoque to adjust aero load limits. Selecting higher downforce packages in the simulator taps into this reserve.

Drag signature lab

Rolling-road wind tunnel data feed the drag penalties per site. Shuttle Landing Facility runs use low yaw, while Nardò’s banking introduces crosswind corrections you must anticipate.

Thermal analytics

Machine-learning models predict battery delta-T when you pick ERS aggression strategies. Exceed the thermal ceiling and the mission will auto-abort with a systems cooling cycle.

Surface reconnaissance

Recon options simulate the tyre temperature blankets, friction surveys, and LiDAR scans the team deploys before a certification pass. Use them to gain grip or resilience at the cost of prep time.
